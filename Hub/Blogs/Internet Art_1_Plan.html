Internet Art Proposal


For my idea this semester, I would like to do something more in line with the works of Mendi Obadike and Miao Ying, and create a piece that is more internet adjacent, than directly formed on the workings of the web itself. Ideally, a piece that can function without the web, but that is enhanced using the web and the interactivity it provides – as in the case of Obadike’s work (Obadike M. L., 2001). However, I also feel like a piece, such as Blind Spot (Ying , 2007), that works in its physicality, influenced by the Web is also something interesting to achieve.

With both works, I really enjoyed the use and spin on words, and examining the way that words work and affect our understanding and how the lack thereof can also limit our understanding.

Therefore, I would like to attempt something that is both physical and digital.

For my project I would like to examine the lens of masculinity and use the revealing hover features from Obadike’s work to comment on the gender binaries that affect the idea of masculinity in the social lens.

I would like to show an image of a predominately neutral or common scene from life – such as a storefront with mannequins or maybe a directory of store names or accessible services and based on whether the interactor picks to be male or female, it will display only what seems to be societally acceptable for them to view, approach or consider, but I want this to show a great focus on what is viewed as unacceptable for men or masculine identifying bodies.

I was very stuck in developing an idea for this project, and I don’t have a lot to offer in terms of the development or planning. However, I would like to pursue this idea more deeply and by the next submission, I would like to have a fully functional prototype that focuses on one scene/place, and that highlights the particular present binaries in that scene. By the end of the course, I would like to present this as a series of three scenes.

In terms of having this be both digital and physical I would like to see if I can directly achieve the scene of the piece through photography of a place here – which would also contextualize the project into the South African context.

Updated (Assignment 2)

Considering the feedback I received, I completely scrapped my initial idea and went back to the drawing board. I was still very inspired by the same works of Mendi Obadike (Obadike, 2001) and Miao Ying (Ying , 2007) and wanted to really incorporate those work into a working unique piece. This time, I wanted to really try and focus on the methodology presented by Ying which I discovered doing extra research (ELEPHANT, 2021), and discussed in my analysis of her work, “Blind Spot” (Pillay, Analysis of ‘Blind Spot’, 2021). In this method, Ying first identifies a core digital aspect and then relates it to a topic, and from there abstracts it into an artwork (ELEPHANT, 2021).

In this way, I first identified the medium I wanted to work with. In trying to bridge the aspects of influence from Ying and Obadike, I noted Ying’s use of the dictionary and from there, the idea sparked to use Urban Dictionary – an influence on whatever work I would make, as it would not exist without the internet, and had not existed before it. The topics I mainly wanted to deal with on this site were from my academic area, and particularly the relations of decolonization, and the need to decolonize and create accessible technology. With the influence of, and constant re-interaction with, Obadike’s work, I decided to pursue something along the line of random generated words, with links that if clicked, would lead to the Urban dictionary definitions for the key terms of my discourse focus for this artwork.

However, implementing this standard link generator did not work in an aesthetic manner, and considering the feedback which classed my site as very detached, I looked for ways to remove the idea of the work being confined to one section. Tackling this problem, I came up with the idea of using a text background that the content of my site would lay over, and then using CSS to hide the text and only have set responses for the key terms, making them links. At first, I really struggled with getting the links to work, and eventually figured that my use of the z-index was incorrect and managed to achieve the work I have up now.

The only major issues I really have with this piece are, first, the About section’s flex nature does not allow it to be affected by the z-index and so I am struggling with the layout I want to achieve – and secondly, this piece functions (as I’ve tried to work with the previous issue) mostly only on desktop and larger screens. The third would be the face that some of the links arent accessibile because they are behind other elemrnts, but with the repeating nautre of most of the ters used, I am not to concerned with this. I am still struggling to address the first issue and it is visible in the way the words and text overlap, but I have decided to make the words somewhat visible on the background and on tap or hover, it becomes less transparent. Although I haven’t had the opportunity to test this, I believe it will, or at least has the potential to breach the mobile issue. Moving forward, I will try to see what I can do about the overlap in the About section.

The text I have used was from my first blog post on the site, “Tackling Decoloniality” (Pillay, Tackling Decoloniality, 2021). Because I want this artwork to focus on decoloniality, and still be very original work, I decided to use my piece and link all the words, even repetitions, to their definitions on Urban dictionary to comment on the way that even though such topics are not new to contemporary culture, they remain relevant, and they are seeked out by the youth and incoming generations. The use of Urban dictionary may initially have been inspired by Ying’s piece, and the final layout by Obadike, but I do believe the commentary that I am trying to achieve about the continuous relevant of such discourse – and showcasing it to interacts stands to work as a piece of internet art.

Although my idea has changed drastically, I hope this piece has provided a clearer scope of my intentions for this piece. As it functions now, it serves to showcase the contemporary relevance of decolonization, and functions as a bridging between the works of Obadike and Ying. Moving forward with this piece, I think I wanted it to function more intersection ally, and by this, I mean to bring in more text pieces from my other work into the layer, and work to identify more terms that relate to decolonial theory in them, regardless of how few terms there may be.
