Technical

In terms of the technical work of my site, I believe I was able to adequately meet the brief.I do believe that in terms of my Homepage contextualizing the project, I could do something more versatile in my Home section, that directly relates to or maybe even is the Internet Art piece when I develop it. However, at this stage, I think it works fine enough to show my work chronologically, and to cover all the necessary components.

In terms of the separate Blog and Internet Art pages, I have instead sectioned them on the homepage to focus on my personal goal of a “one page” site. Outside of the separate blog pages – which I eventually hope to show as pop-ups if possible – the only page I feel might really get a separate page, is the Internet Art Project itself. Currently, I have not started with the development of it but, I have started a section and a blog that details my plans for moving forward.

I have made use of good semantic markup, metadata – although I believe I should vary it more per page – and my site is responsive, except for some few elements, namely my navigation arrows on my homepage. I have also used CSS quite heavily to arrange and finesse my site and used minimal JS to create a more seamless responsive design.

Theoretical

I have included all the necessary blog posts topics but have decided to merge the technical and artistic reflections into one living document that will be updated with each assignment. It details all the decisions made and will elaborate more reflectively on the work done on the site.

For my close reading and analysis, I am quite happy with my work, as I have finally started to correctly reference my ideas and really involve myself with more than just the provided texts. Moving forward, I would definitely like to produce more work and really make the theoretical aspect/approach to my work more dominate in this project, outside of the main Internet Art component.

Overall, I currently believe this work is my strongest of the three marking sections

Creative Practice

I do think my website has good UI practices – but they can still be improved, especially in terms of accessibility. I think the aesthetic works well, but as compared to previous site developments, I was not as considerate in my design, and want to address this moving forward. I believe there might be some small aesthetic changes - sizing, and the like, taken into consideration by the next submission.

I do think my website’s content is coherent and really showcases my focus on decoloniality. Retrospectively, I now see the brief I have set out for my project may seem a bit detached in its feminism focus lens, as compared to my analysis and readings which are more toward the racial-decolonial direction.

I do not think it is a train smash, and I think the idea I am proposing could still be used in that direction, with some considerations and a different focus. For my internet art, I have only recently really begun to develop my idea, but have not yet made any progress on it’s development nor have I really made space to see how it would resonate with my site’s aesthetic – but, noting what I mentioned previously, I believe I will be making some changes to the focus of the project, and shift from a gender based approach, to a more racial one that correlates more with my focus of my theoretical direction.
