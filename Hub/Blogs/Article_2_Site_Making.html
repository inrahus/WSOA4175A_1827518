Site Making


For this site making, I was more focused on developing a working responsive site with more theoretical focus - as compared to last year, where I really played around with colour, visuals and experimented with CSS and JavaScript to create different and interesting engagement with the user (Pillay, 2020). This year, I wanted to simplify the website’s aesthetic, and focus on a minimalistic palette, and work more on my site content than the display. Despite the visual of the site not being as important to me this time around, I have one main goal – and that is to contain most of the site to a single page – the home page.

For my goal, I would like it if everything could be done on one page – however, I will initially try to cut down on excessive intermediaries – like a separate blog collections page or arts collections page – and make it all accessible from the home screen. The code I used in my vis.co site (Pillay, vis.co - Website Design, 2020), to section the homepage, allows for this, but I did not make such good use of it, and would like to attempt it this time around.

During the initial design of the site, I reverted to my other site works on GitHub, and reviewed what I enjoyed and didn’t enjoy from my previous work. From the time of my last academic site, I also made a mock homepage for a company of a relative who’s company’s site used to be quite awful, to develop my skills and test out different codes and styles (Pillay, Home, 2020). I used different components and code from both the TSI site and my vis.co site (Pillay, vis.co - Website Design, 2020) from last year, to create and change the display of my site, while also prioritizing the responsivity of the site and it’s display on mobile. However, as it stands, there are some issues with the mobile display – such as the directional arrows sizing, which I have yet to address, but hope to before the submission.

Another issue I am currently facing is with my full page Nav Bar – where the animation displays below the whole bar, but not under each link – but when I test the responsivity on mobile view, it works and when I revert to the full screen, it also suddenly works. I believe I will need to apply the animation to each link individually for this to be fixed but will do so at a later stage.

Since I based this site off my two previously made sites, I did not follow the method of wireframe mapping and instead used a more template-based method. Because of this, currently I am worried that my site may seem too much like my site from last year. I don’t know yet how to reference correctly in terms of the coding, but I hope to have done the adequate research necessary for the second assignment submission.

In terms of my theoretical focus, I would like to – as I have done for Tackling Decoloniality – focus on engaging more with the close readings, analysis, and reflections, which I haven’t really done as well before. By this, I mean to really treat each more like mini-essays and do the additional work to support my points – as opposed to my previous work, which was more comparable to literature reviews. I also want to fucus on referencing more correctly and really recognizing where different works have influenced my positions.

With regards to the use of React.JS - I have yet to make use of this and have, for the time begin, stuck majorly to html and css for my layout and design, with a slight use of JS for the responsivity of my site.

With each submission, I plan to update this document, as a more reflective piece, compared to the report which I would like to tackle more objectively.

Updated (Assignment 2)

With the feedback I received for my site, and especially my internet artwork, I knew that I really need to re-evaluate some aspects of my site, and maybe create more of a flow to the site. This was overall, secondary to me, and most of the changes in my site were results of pursuing my internet artwork, and this is further discussed in my Internet Art Post.

In efforts to, after these artistic changes, really value my feedback, I tried to address the lack of flow in my site and focus on minimal changes that would still allow me to keep my one-page site. The first of which was changing my “Latest” section, to “Updated and Ongoing”, so that content would not repeat in my blog section and would create more of a spacious look to my page. It also benefitted my internet artwork and allowed for more of the links to be access and seen by the interactors of the site, while also giving a clear direction of which works on my site I would be working on continuously, and which I have deemed completed.

I kept my “About” section the same and intending on treating it the same say I do my Site Making and Internet Artwork post – as living documents that are updated with each submission. These aspects of my site, unlike the content I am storing in the Blog sections are everchanging and are based on my own thoughts, feelings, and the feedback I receive with each submission. I also feel that showing these first on my site, allows it to be well contextualized in term of our assignment briefs. The reason I chose to follow up my About section after this instead of my Blog section, is because I really prefer it to be there. Although it would make sense, contextually and narratively to place it right at the end of the site, I hate how it looks. That is why I have given it its own risen bubble case, to show it’s break in flow, and that it is something that can be looked at later.

In terms of what I haven’t addressed or fixed on my site, this would most likely be the Nav Bar. Both the underlining issue that I mentioned previously, I had no time to address, and now, with the removal of the “Art” section to really implement my art idea, the spacing is very off and annoying. I hope to address this swiftly by the final submission. I also want to, as I mentioned in my Internet Art post, really work on the mobile scaling of the site, particularly in terms of the piece. It’s a lot of playing around with responsive max widths and text sizes, so I am hoping that it will not be a huge issue to tackle going forward.

The last thing to talk about is the implementation of React.js. In my site, I focused mainly on use of HTML and CSS because it’s what I know, and it’s what I’m good at. I have tried to follow along with Andre’s tutorials, some successful and some not so successful. However, in terms of the site I am presenting, I currently find no need to implement the React.js. Even in terms of my Internet Art, I can achieve what I want, albeit less convenient, in a way that I understand and can explain – I can’t do that with React.js yet. I am still open to the idea of implementing it and hope to maybe gain insight into how it could help me tackle some of the issues I have already presented above.

Overall, minimal changes have been made to my site, but in this piece, I hope I have given more insight and clarified a lot of the previously confusing decisions I made. I also hope that the changes have given a better flow to the site, and that the issues I mentioned will be resolvable by the time I submit the final project. In terms of React.js, I think my stance on why I haven’t implemented it is clear, but moving forward, I will try to see how it can further the development of my site.
